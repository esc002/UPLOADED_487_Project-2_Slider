What is Tokidoki?

Tokidoki is a fashion and lifestyle brand inspired by Japanese popular culture and art. The company was created in 2005 by Italian artist Simone Legno. He had always loved Japanese culture since he was first introduced to Japanese culture through anime in the 90s. What is Kawaii?

According to Merriam-Webster Dictionary; "In Japanese, the word kawaii has a meaning that sits more or less at the juncture of "cute," "tiny," or "lovable." The cute aesthetic‚Äîwith its bold, nearly cartoon-like lines and rounded forms‚Äîinforms a large segment of Japanese popular culture." (Merriam-Webster Dictionary)

"Kawaii", meaning "cute" in Japanese, could be described as a movement in Japanese culture. Cuteness emerged from post-war Japan's rapid economic growth in the 1970s due to rapid commercialization. Since then, Japan has become immensely influential in popular culture around the globe. "Kawaii", according to Sato's "From Hello Kitty to Cod Roe Kewpie", has also become a form of self-expression and subversion. An example of this is Tokidoki's frequent inclusion of punk elements, sharp spikes, bright colors, and traditional paintings (some of which you can see in the background!).

Tokidoki Characters

Tokidoki's franchises range from the more traditionally "kawaii" sweets-themed "Donutella" franchise, to less conventional themes like the cactus themed "Cactus Friends" or the punk inspired "punkstar" franchise. Most notable is the "Unicorno" series, which includes series of unicorn collectibles of unique designs, many drawing from Japanese culture. Tokidoki's iconic characters decorate various products such clothing or accessories in addition to figures.

Tokidoki Collabs

In addition to Japanese culture, some likely influences on Tokidoki include: Japanese mascots such as Hello Kitty, fashion sketch illustrations, and manga. Since it's formation, Tokidoki has collaborated with countless brands in these areas including Sanrio, haute couture brands and icons, manga, conventions, and more. Some notable examples include Karl Lagerfeld: a fashion designer, Sanrio: the company behind Hello Kitty, and Japanese vocaloid singer: Hatsune Miku.

Conclusion

Tokidoki's success indicates the explosion in popularity of Japanese popular culture, especially "kawaii". Tokidoki's success with older audiences shows that "kawaii" appeals to people of all ages. Furthermore, Tokidoki's tendency to draw from various global cultures emphasizes the globalization of today's pop culture and art.
