Tokidoki

What is Tokidoki?

Tokidoki is a fashion and lifestyle brand inspired by Japanese popular culture and art. The company was created in 2005 by Italian artist Simone Legno. He was first introduced to Japanese culture through anime in the 90s. "Tokidoki" means "sometimes" in Japanese, which Legno explains is because "everyone waits for moments that change one‚Äôs destiny, by chance or by meeting a new person".

What is Kawaii?

"Kawaii", meaning "cute" in Japanese, has grown into a cultural movement with roots in post-war Japan. Since then, Japan has become immensely influential in popular culture. In addition to its marketing strength, "kawaii" has also become a movement. According to Sato's "From Hello Kitty to Cod Roe Kewpie", cuteness can be subverted as a tool of self-expression and resistance. This idea is prevalent in Tokidoki's inclusion of punk elements and subversive designs.

Tokidoki Characters

Tokidoki's franchises range from a more traditionally "kawaii" sweets-themed "Donutella" franchise, to less conventional themes like a cactus themed "Cactus Friends" or the punk inspired "punkstar". Most notable is the "Unicorno" series, which includes series of unicorn collectibles with very unique designs, many drawing from Japanese culture. Tokidoki's iconic characters decorate various products such clothing or accessories in addition to figures.

Tokidoki Collabs

In addition to Japanese culture, some likely influences on Tokidoki include: Japanese mascots such as Hello Kitty, fashion sketch illustrations, and manga. Since it's formation, Tokidoki has collaborated with countless brands in these areas including Sanrio, haute couture brands and icons, manga, conventions, and more. Some notable examples include Karl Lagerfeld: a fashion designer, Sanrio: the company behind Hello Kitty, and Japanese vocaloid singer: Hatsune Miku.

Conclusion

Tokidoki's success indicates the explosion in popularity of Japanese popular culture, especially "kawaii". Tokidoki's success with older audiences shows that "kawaii" isn't strictly for kids, and can be used as a form of self expression. Furthermore, Tokidoki's tendency to draw from various global cultures emphasizes the globalization of today's pop culture and art.
